Inspirations: FTL

- Very successful game, you might have heard about it - If not, make sure you've got a lot of free time before trying it. Don't say I didn't warn you. - Very different game from what Von Neumann Defense Force attempts to be on a surface level, but it does a lot of things right. - Constant forward movement. Player is constantly pressured. - Ups and downs. You never now what is around the corner. - This makes for interesting stories. - This isn't a game where you try one thing a hundred times until you manage to pass, then move on to the next thing. - While VNDF will look nothing like FTL, I want to replicate this feeling of adventure, the telling of your own stories. - VNDF won't be a game where you go back and forth for hours, accumulating whatever it is you need. - Any attempt to gain something will require you to risk what you already have. - You won't be able to climb steadily in VNDF. - Your time playing the game will be an adventure with ups and downs, and that will make it all the more interesting.

In Other News

As always, I'm going to close the newsletter with some space- and gaming-related news from around the Internet.

JAXA has successfully launched the Hayabusa 2 mission! If everything goes to plan, the craft will arrive at its target, the asteroid (162173) 1999 JU3, in 2018, land there and return surface samples back to earth in 2020.

NASA has completed a very successful first test flight of its new Orion capsule. During the 4-hour flight, the unmanned capsule reached a distance of about 5800 kilometers from earth, the farthest any human-rated spacecraft has gone since the last Apollo mission in 1972! I'm not so sure that this really is the first step towards a manned Mars mission, but it's certainly exciting that NASA is regaining its capability of sending humans beyond low Earth orbit.
