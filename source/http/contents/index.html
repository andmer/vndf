So, you've probably played some sandbox games before, right? Something like Minecraft or Starbound? Well, I have and I love it! The freedom, the adventure, the limitless potential! You won't believe the hours I've spent in those games just exploring and building! (then again, maybe you will)

But it's not all ponies and roses with those sandbox games! As I keep building and exploring, I always reach the point, where I look at what I'm doing and notice that what used to be fun somehow has turned into work. Not that I have anything against work. I love work! But even though I love it, work is not really what I'm looking for in a computer game! Especially, if it's not the fun kind of work.

So what is it that turns these games from fun into work? I can't really tell what would do that for you, but I know what does it for me: Challenge or rather, the lack thereof. To this day, I like to remember that Minecraft server me and my friend played on: We spawned in the ocean, found a tiny island and almost starved to death before we managed to craft a fishing rod and caught some fish, literally in the last minute.

We survived thanks to tenacity and teamwork. It was awesome! Later we built a huge castle with all kinds of stuff in it. At some point, I got bored and stopped playing.

What I want is challenge. Do you know a game that can give me that? I know one! It's FTL. I think I love FTL even more than I love Minecraft. Not only is it difficult it also forces me to make harsh decisions. (Is this reference to this site's title a sign that this detour is about to end? Oh yes it is! In just a few more long-winded paragraphs...)

FTL will offer you this awesome gun that may help you win the game, but guess what, if you buy it, you won't have any money left to repair your damaged hull. Will you buy the weapon, and maybe die in the next fight? Or will you repair your hull and die later, when your weaponry will be too underpowered to do anything against the tougher enemies in the later sectors?

Maybe you'll survive the next few fights and maybe you'll have another change to repair your hull then. Or maybe you can get a really good weapon later, before the enemies become too tough. The thing with FTL is, you don't know, but you have to decide now and there are no do-overs. This one decision can lead you to death or to victory. And either way, you will be the only one to blame for it.

By constantly challenging you, constantly driving you forward, FTL makes you write your own memorable stories. But as good as FTL is, it is not a sandbox game. It also won't let you play with your friends. I want to play a game that lets me do all those things, without any of the downsides!

So what exactly do we want? (Yes, I'm saying we. You're part of this now.) Let's reiterate.

  • We want an open-world sandbox game that evokes feelings of freedom and adventure.
  • But we also want to be challenged! We want a game that forces us to make harsh decisions , thereby enabling us to write our own story.
  • This game must be a multiplayer game, so we can share that experience with our friends. You know what, while we're at it, let's make it a full-blown MMO!
  • And because we love science and space, we want this to be a science fiction game. Not any old sci-fi, no, we want hard science. Unless this game forces us to learn how to do a Hohmann transfer or how to get a capture by use of aerobraking, then we won't even bother!
